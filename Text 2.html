Adolf Hitler remains one of the most reviled figures in world history due to his role in causing the deaths of millions during World War II, particularly the Holocaust. His regime's genocidal policies, aggressive territorial expansion, and racial hatred left an indelible mark on the 20th century and led to the loss of tens of millions of lives. His actions led to the collapse of Nazi Germany and the establishment of a new world order, with the United Nations, the European Union, and other international bodies created in the aftermath of the war to prevent future global conflicts.
